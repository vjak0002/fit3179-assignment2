☺ World Happiness Report ☺

World Happiness Report is a survey of the state of global happiness that ranks 146 countries by how happy their citizens perceive themselves to be. The Happiness Index is a score between 0 and 10, with 10 being the happiest. The report is based on answers to the main life evaluation question asked in the poll. The question was worded as follows: "Please imagine a ladder, with steps numbered from 0 at the bottom to 10 at the top. The top of the ladder represents the best possible life for you and the bottom of the ladder represents the worst possible life for you. On which step of would you say you personally feel you stand at this time?"

North America, Northern Europe and Oceania seem to be the happiest. Africa overall is below the average world happiness index (~5.5), we expect this as these countries have a low GDP per capita and a low quality of life. China is an interesting situation, it is growing economically but the happiness of its citizens is not increasing. This could be due to the fact that the Chinese government is not doing enough to improve the income inequality and the happiness of its citizens.

Dystopia + Residual ⓘ
Dystopia is a combination of two Greek words 'dys' meaning bad and 'topos' meaning place. In this context, Dystopia refers to an imaginary country that has the unhappiest people in the world. Residual is the unexplained variance in the Happiness Index.
Freedom: Life Choices ⓘ
Freedom is the average of the responses to the question: "Are you satisfied or dissatisfied with your freedom to choose what you do with your life?"
GDP per capita ⓘ
GDP is the average of the responses to the question: "How would you rate your standard of living?"
Generosity ⓘ
Generosity is the average of the responses to the question: "Have you donated money to a charity in the past month?"
Healthy Life Expectancy ⓘ
Life Expectancy is the average of the responses to the question: "How many years do you expect to live?"
Perceptions of Corruption ⓘ
Corruption is the average of the responses to the question: "Is corruption widespread throughout the government or not?"
Social Support ⓘ
Social Support is the average of the responses to the question: "If you were in trouble, do you have relatives or friends you can count on to help you whenever you need them, or not?"
80% of the countries in the bottom 10 are in Africa. This is a major problem as African resources are being exploited by other countries for centuries now and have fuelled state corruption, violence and poverty.
Afganistan is the least happy country in the world. This is mainly due to the war and government corruption that has been going on for the past 40 years forcing people below povery line, inconsistent development and lack of jobs.
As seen from these charts, no single factor can explain the happiness of a country. When analyzing happiness, we need to account for factors like income inequality, corruption, freedom, social support, life expectancy and generosity.
GDP is a driving force for all other factors since money can influence factors like healthier life, freedom to make life choices, freedom from corrpution, income inequality, and overall peace levels.

Developed countries are expected to retain their happiness levels if not improve it as the governments are continuously investing in new technologies like AI, Renewable Energy, Electric Cars, etc. On the flip side, poor and unhappy countries could get worse day by day as they have limited resources, poor infrastructure and lack of education. Climate change could also show its effects very quickly in these countries.

To conclude, developed and happy countries will most likely retain their happiness levels and poor and unhappy countries will most likely get worse or remain same due to their circumstances. GDP per capita is a powerful factor that that contributes to happiness of a country, but not the only one.
